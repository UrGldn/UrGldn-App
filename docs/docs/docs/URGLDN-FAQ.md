# URGLDN – Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## 1. What is URGLDN?
URGLDN is a timing‑first financial wellness tool that locks bill money ahead of time and releases it automatically at the exact moment a payment is due. It prevents overdrafts, late fees, and timing failures by protecting funds before they can be accidentally spent.

---

## 2. How does the Lock & Release™ system work?
Users link their existing bank accounts through secure APIs.  
URGLDN creates “vaults” for each bill:

- Money is **locked early**  
- Funds are **protected** from accidental spending  
- Money is **released automatically** at the exact due time  

This ensures bills are always paid on time without stress or surprises.

---

## 3. Does URGLDN require users to switch banks?
No.  
URGLDN integrates with the banking experience people already use.  
It works alongside existing accounts, auto‑pay setups, and bill payment systems.

---

## 4. Who is URGLDN designed for?
URGLDN is built for anyone who struggles with:

- Bill timing  
- Overspending  
- Fixed income management  
- Irregular pay cycles  
- Financial stress  
- Disability‑related challenges  
- PTSD or cognitive load issues  
- Busy schedules and forgetfulness  

It is especially helpful for people who want structure, automation, and peace of mind.

---

## 5. What problem does URGLDN solve?
URGLDN solves **timing failures**, not budgeting failures.

Most financial stress comes from:

- Bills hitting before payday  
- Forgotten payments  
- Auto‑pay pulling when accounts aren’t ready  
- Small shortfalls causing big consequences  

URGLDN eliminates these issues by handling timing automatically.

---

## 6. What makes URGLDN different from autopay?
Autopay pulls money **on the due date**, whether the account is ready or not.  
URGLDN locks the money **before** the due date and releases it at the perfect moment.

Autopay reacts.  
URGLDN prepares.

---

## 7. Is URGLDN a budgeting app?
No.  
URGLDN is a **timing tool**, not a budgeting tool.

It doesn’t categorize spending or track purchases.  
It protects bill money and ensures payments happen on time.
